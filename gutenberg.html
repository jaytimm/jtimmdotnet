In these excerpts, the repetition of "She could always" unveils a compelling portrait of a woman's steadfastness and reliability, echoing the myriad ways in which she navigates her world. This phrase pattern reveals not just her consistency but also the deep interweaving of memory, duty, and talent, suggesting that her strength lies in both the mundane and extraordinary aspects of life. Through these snapshots, we glimpse how her character is shaped by the moments she cherishes, the challenges she faces, and the roles she embraces.

  • She could always recall in detail a September Sunday afternoon, when they had sat under the vine at the back of her father’s house. | Lawrence, D. H.
  • She could always face a situation when it admitted the possibility of anything being done, when there was a chance for resolution and action. | Duncan, Sara Jeannette
  • She could always come back, when the detestable French had gone away home, and live with her friend again. | McFee, William
  • She could always be depended on to protect the interests of an absent child. | Fillmore, Parker
  • She could always tell when it was eleven o’clock, by the clatter of the teacups. | Ruskay, Sophie
  • She could always gather a large and admiring audience at her literary conferences. | Pryor, Sara Agnes Rice
  • She could always spell correctly without the aid of a Book, while to me the spelling lesson was the hardest of tasks. | Sears, John Van der Zee
